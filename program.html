Contest Programs

Pre Contest preparation

This is the broader phase that typically starts weeks or months before the actual competition. The focus is on building muscle mass, improving symmetry, and reducing body fat to achieve a well-defined and aesthetic physique. Training intensity may increase, and nutrition is carefully monitored to support muscle growth while gradually decreasing body fat.

Contest Preparation

The contest preparation phase occurs closer to the actual competition, often in the final weeks or months. The primary goal shifts to achieving maximum muscle definition and reducing body fat to showcase the sculpted physique on stage. Nutrition becomes more strict, with attention to macronutrient balance and fluid manipulation to enhance muscle visibility and vascularity. Training may involve specific techniques to "peak" the muscles, emphasizing details such as muscle striations and separation.

Posing Routine

A posing routine in bodybuilding refers to the choreographed display of a competitor's physique on stage during a competition.

Post Contest Therapy - PCT

The role of a trainer in Post Contest Therapy (PCT) involves providing support and guidance to individuals transitioning from the intense pre-competition phase to a more balanced lifestyle.

Fat loss

Weight loss

Strength Training

Healing Program

Injury to healing

"Injury to healing" refers to the process of transitioning from a state of physical harm or damage (injury) to a state of recovery and restoration (healing).
